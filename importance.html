Why Forests Matter

The Forest Conservation Campaign is all about protecting our nature and conserving all aspects of it for our future as well as for our next generations.

Forests play a crucial part in sustaining life on our planet. Around 1.6 billion people including more than 2,000 indigenous cultures depend on forests for their livelihoods, medicines, fuel, food and shelter. Forests are the most biologically diverse ecosystems on land, home to more than 80% of the terrestrial species of animals, plants and insects.

There has been a drastic fall in the total area covered by forests due to agricultural expansion, logging, mining, and development. Controlling deforestation is the need of the hour. We must contribute in protecting the forests. The government has implemented The Forest (Conservation) Act, 1980, for the same.

The Forest Conservation Campaign focuses on:

  • Plant trees: Plant trees in your local area and encourage others to do the same.
  • Sustainable forestry: Control logging to prevent overexploitation and ensure forest regeneration.
  • Legislation and enforcement: Enforce strict laws against illegal logging and create protected areas.
  • Reduce consumption: Minimize your use of paper and wood products. Choose sustainable options.
